FAREWELL, CHAMPIONS

Team India lifts the ICC T20 World Cup 2024, ending an 11-year ICC trophy drought. From dominance in the group stage to composure in the final — this was a complete team performance.

Rohit Sharma led from the front, setting the tone in the powerplay with aggressive starts. His knock against Australia was a tournament highlight. Truly a captain’s performance.

When it mattered most, Virat delivered. Anchoring India in the final, he silenced critics with a composed, match-winning fifty. A perfect farewell to T20I greatness.

Returning after injury, Pant showed grit and maturity. Agile behind the stumps and crucial in finishing lower-order innings, he proved his worth once again.

Known for his fearless strokeplay, SKY’s acrobatic catch in the final became an instant classic. He kept the scoreboard ticking and electrified the field.

Axar delivered under pressure with bat and ball. His explosive cameo in the final turned the game, and his tight overs held momentum.

Dube played the role of finisher with brute force. His six-hitting ability gave India the edge in crunch moments during the middle and death overs.

From finishing the innings with the bat to bowling the nerve-wracking last over, Hardik was calm, focused, and deadly accurate. A true all-round star.

He might not have flashy numbers, but Jadeja’s fielding, spin control, and quick cameos made a huge difference in tight situations.

Kuldeep’s clever wrist spin bamboozled top-order batsmen. His spell in the semifinal turned the tide and kept India in control.

Arshdeep struck early in most games, delivering breakthroughs when needed. His inswingers and yorkers made him lethal in the first six.

Bumrah was near-unplayable. Whether it was the powerplay or death overs, he broke partnerships and built pressure. India’s true MVP.

Coach Dravid's silent leadership and strategic vision shaped this young squad into champions. His farewell couldn't be more perfect.

Two icons of Indian cricket signed off from T20Is on the ultimate high. Their names are forever etched in history, their final act a storybook ending.
